Our Story

About Edge
Camouflage

A research lab and open-source platform born out of MIT CSAIL. We study how AI systems can be fooled — so they can be made more robust.

Mission
We believe understanding attack is prerequisite to building defense.

Edge Camouflage was founded on the premise that the adversarial ML community needs shared, open infrastructure — standardized attacks, reproducible benchmarks, and tools that work the same way in a MIT lab as in a production AI safety team.

We are deeply committed to responsible disclosure, dual-use research ethics, and keeping our core tools freely available to academic researchers worldwide.

History

From MIT lab
to global platform

2021
Founded at MIT CSAIL
Edge Camouflage begins as a graduate research group studying evasion attacks on medical imaging models. First paper accepted to ICLR 2022.
2022
EC-Attack Library v1.0 open-sourced
The first open release of EC tooling, supporting PyTorch models with FGSM and PGD. 1,000 GitHub stars in the first week.
2023
DARPA GARD partnership announced
Edge Camouflage joins the DARPA Guaranteeing AI Robustness against Deception (GARD) program. Physical-world attack research begins.
2024
ICLR Spotlight & platform launch
Flagship EC paper receives ICLR 2024 Spotlight. Platform launches with REST API, Python/JS SDKs, and MIT co-certified training programs.
2025
12K researchers, 6 languages
Community crosses 12,000 members. LLM red-teaming track launches. EC Researcher Hub goes live as a unified open resource portal.
Partnership

Rooted at MIT

Edge Camouflage was founded within MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL). Our work is conducted in collaboration with faculty advisors, PhD students, and postdoctoral researchers at MIT across multiple research groups.

EC certification programs are co-developed with MIT and recognized for academic credit in MIT's 6.S978 (Adversarial Machine Learning) course. We maintain formal affiliations with MIT Lincoln Laboratory and the MIT AI Policy Forum.

Ethics

Our ethics policy

Adversarial research is inherently dual-use. We take our responsibility seriously.

🔏
Disclosure
Coordinated Disclosure
We follow a strict 90-day coordinated disclosure policy for any attack that targets specific deployed systems. Affected parties are notified before public release.
🚫
Access Control
Verified Research Access
The most powerful EC tools require institutional verification. We manually review access requests to prevent misuse by non-research actors.
