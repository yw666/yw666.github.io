Core Values

How we operate

Six principles that guide every paper we publish, every tool we ship, and every disclosure we make.

01
Openness by Default
All EC tools, datasets, and code are open-source under MIT license. Science advances through transparency, not secrecy.
02
Responsible Disclosure
We follow coordinated vulnerability disclosure for every AI system vulnerability we discover. We never exploit — we inform.
03
Rigor First
Every claim we make is backed by peer-reviewed experiments with published code. Reproducibility is non-negotiable.
04
Dual-Use Awareness
We take seriously the dual-use nature of adversarial research. Every release is reviewed by our ethics board for potential misuse.
05
Community Over Competition
We collaborate with other adversarial ML labs rather than competing. The shared goal — safe AI — is larger than any single lab.
06
Defense & Offense Together
We publish attack research and certified defense research in equal measure. You cannot defend what you do not understand.

History

How we got here

From a two-person MIT thesis project to a global research lab.

2019
Founded at MIT CSAIL
Dr. Maya Chen and Dr. Ravi Nair begin EC as a side project during their MIT PhDs, publishing the first EC preprint on arXiv.
2021
EC-Attack Library v1.0
First public release of the EC-Attack Python library. 1,000 GitHub stars within the first week.
2022
DARPA Award & Lab Expansion
$4.2M DARPA AI Exploration award. Lab expands to 12 researchers. First physical-world attack paper published at CVPR.
2024
ICLR Spotlight & 10K Users
Flagship EC paper receives ICLR 2024 Spotlight. Platform reaches 10,000 registered researchers globally.
2025
EC v3.0 — LLM Red-Teaming
Major platform release adding full LLM red-teaming support. MIT certification program launches with 500+ initial enrollments.
