Research Areas

What we study

Core research pillars driving the Edge Camouflage agenda at MIT and beyond.

01
Evasion Attacks on Vision Models
Generating imperceptible perturbations that fool state-of-the-art image classifiers, detectors, and segmentation models under white-box and black-box constraints.
02
Physical-World Adversarial Patches
Designing printed adversarial patches that persist under real-world conditions: lighting variation, viewpoint changes, and compression artifacts.
03
LLM Red-Teaming & Jailbreaking
Automated adversarial prompt generation to expose safety misalignments in instruction-tuned language models at scale.
04
Transferability & Black-Box Attacks
Exploiting the phenomenon by which adversarial examples crafted for one model transfer to fool others, enabling attacks without model access.
05
Certified Robustness & Defenses
Developing provably robust defenses using randomized smoothing, adversarial training, and formal verification methods.
06
Multimodal & Cross-Modal Attacks
Adversarial attacks on vision-language systems (CLIP, LLaVA, GPT-4V) using synchronized perturbations across image and text modalities.
